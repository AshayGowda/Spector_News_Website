Skipper Virat Kohli slammed his career-best Test score of 254 as India posted a mammoth 601/5 before declaring on Day 2 of the second Test against South Africa. India have declared their innings for a record 24th time under the captaincy of Virat Kohli. This is the most by any international team since Kohli took over as India Test captain. In this period, Australia and England have declared their innings 18 times, which is the second-highest after India. In the process, Kohli smashed his seventh double ton and went past Virender Sehwag, Sachin Tendulkar for the most double hundreds in Test cricket by an Indian.

Virat Kohli scored his first double-century against West Indies in June 2016 in his 42nd Test. This means that Kohli has scored seven Test double-centuries in a span of 40 matches. Since June 2016, no other Test team has scored more than three double-centuries. England, New Zealand and Pakistan have hit three each whereas Australia and Bangladesh have scored two with West Indies getting one double century. If we look at individual feats, no other batsman has scored more than two double-centuries since June 2016. Azhar Ali, Alastair Cook and Steven Smith have hit two each. Kohli also became the third cricketer after Sri Lanka's Kumar Sangakkara and Younis Khan of Pakistan to hit double tons against six different Test playing nations.
